MR 4800: Special Topics in Meteorology -- Data-Driven Weather Prediction (3CR)
Meeting Times: Mon. - Wed.: 1000-1050
Location: Root 117/123

Instructor: Scott Powell
Email: scott dot powell at nps dot edu
Office: Root 255


Final Project: Due Date TBD in March.


Course objectives:

- Explain the differences between different neural network architectures used in weather and climate prediction.

- Assess and describe limits of predictability of data-driven ML weather prediction models.

- Assess and describe ensemble dispersiveness of data-driven ML ensemble weather prediction systems.

- Articulate the strengths and weaknesses of traditional NWP and ML weather prediction at forecasting extreme weather events.

- Describe methods used to generate ML ensembles for weather prediction.

- Explain ways to understand how to link ML predictions and failures to real processes occurring in the atmosphere.

Textbooks: None
Grading: All assignments must be completed. An incomplete will be given for a final grade if any assignments are not completed without approval from the instructor.
Weights for final grade are as follows: Forecasts: 20%, Leading paper discussion: 20%, Midterm Project: 30%, Final Project: 30%.
